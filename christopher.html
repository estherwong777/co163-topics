Christopher Gunadi

About me

Hi, I'm Christopher, you can call me Chris! I am a first year Computing undegraduate at Imperial College London. A little bit about myself: I'm from Jakarta, Indonesia and I enjoy swimming, playing badminton, travelling and listening to music amongst other things. My absolute favourite place to go is Japan as I've always been fascinated by how detail-oriented and intricate the people and culture are - they have a product for nearly anything! I speak English, Indonesian, Chinese and a bit of Japanese. Throughout my life, I have always been interested in maths and problem-solving. When doing A Levels, my teacher introduced me to the idea of taking computer science as a course. I read about different algorithms and picked up basic python to solve Project Euler questions and instantly fell in love with the logic associated in writing code. My current technical background include working with Java, Haskell and SQL.

Why this topic?

I am relatively new to most areas of study in Computing, but computer graphics struck me as one of the most interesting as we see the impact of computer graphics almost everywhere - from movies to games. Learning about the different models used to simulate what we see in the real world using computing power is really eye-opening for me. It showed me that there really is limitless ways of thinking about the same problem and that improvements can always be made - as shown in more advanced models being developed in more recent years. Moreover, I particularly enjoyed the crossover with Physics. While reading the research papers, I realised that there was significant Physics involved such as conservation of energy to ensure that the rendered images were physically correct and realistic.
